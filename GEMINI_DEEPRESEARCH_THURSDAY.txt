BEAT 0 — THE VITALS (outdoor-decision dashboard)
Boiseans open the newsletter to answer one question: "can I do my thing outside today?" Research the live numbers, with sources, for a six-cell dashboard. Two anchors every issue:
* Weather — today's high/low and a one-phrase sky read.
* Daylight — today's sunrise and sunset times.
Then the FOUR cells that matter THIS season:
* Spring: Foothills trail condition (Ridge to Rivers — dry/soft/muddy), Boise River flow (cfs + water temp, USGS gauge), pollen level, air quality (AQI).
* Summer: Air quality + wildfire smoke (AQI), Boise River (cfs/temp + float-ability), fire restrictions + UV index, afternoon foothills heat.
* Fall: Foothills trail condition, cottonwood/foliage status, first-frost / overnight-low watch, air quality (AQI).
* Winter: Bogus Basin (lifts open + new snow), valley inversion + air quality, road/pass conditions (Bogus Rd, I-84), overnight low.
For each cell give: a short headline value (e.g. "1,140 cfs", "Soft", "AQI 38"), a one-line sub-detail, and a STATUS — good (go enjoy it) / neutral / caution (doable with a caveat) / alert (don't, or plan around it).
Finish with ONE plain-language ADVISORY sentence that turns the data into a decision (e.g. "Smoke rolls in fast this week — run before noon and float the river early"). Mark it info, caution, or alert.
